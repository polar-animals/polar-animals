Massive, strong with killing instincts, polar bears are tough predators in a harash enviroment. They are at the top of the food chain and their diet is entirely meat based. Quite fearsome but still tender and loving to their sweet precious cubs.

Size

Adults polar bear weight about 680kg and are 2.5m long. This makes them the largest carnivores on earth.

Skin

Polar Bears have white fur but black skin which helps soaking up sunlight to be efficent.

Range

In the Arctic - norhon parts of Alaska and Russia, Greenland and Canada.

Population

According to The World Conservation Union, polar bears population is between 20 000 and 25 000.

Breeding

Usually in April and May. Gestation period lasts approximently 8 months.

Activness

Unlike most of their bear mates, polar bears do not hibernate.

Diet

Their favourite prays are seals since they contain enought fat. Also they consume walrus and carcasses bird's eggs.

Lifespan

Usially 26 to 29 years.

Enemies?

Polar bears have no natural enemies. Only humans could be a treat to them

Rolling

They roll into the snow in order to clean or cool themselves.

Swimming

Polar bears are excelent swimmers. They can swim 100 kilometers (60 miles) without having to take a rest.

Cubs

The cubs (1 to 3) spent the winter in a den protected by their mother. On spring they are taught how to hunt.
