Arctic Fox

Being a tiny animal in an environment with extremely low temperatures, lack of food and tough predators isn't an easy job. Small and clever, Arctic fox manages to resist the harsh field and continue to be interesting to explore and adorable.

Fur

Fur Arctic foxes change their fur colour according to the season. In the winter they have white fur while in the summer - brown.

Diet

Lemmings are their main diet. But they would eat anything that is available in the frozen tundra due to the cold climate.

Litters

Both father and mather stay together to raise their children. Litters are usually 5 to 8 but can be more than 20.

Partners

Arctic foxed are monogamous animals and have only one partner for live.

Lifespan

Usually from 3 to 4 years in the wild and up to 14 years in captivity.

Range

They live in the arctic and can found in Canada, Alaska, Greenland, Iceland, northern parts of Russia and Scandinavia.

Enemies

Except humans Wolves, Golden eagles, and Polar bears are treat. Youngsters are not always safe in the den since they could be dug out

Breeding

Arctic foxes give birth usually in the spring but they start searching for partner in the winter.

Size

Adult males weight averagely 3.5 kg (7.7 lb) while and females - 2.9 kg (6.4 lb). Small size makes them pray to other animals.

Endurance

Endurance Thick fur and excellent heating system allow arctic foxes to not shiver unless temperature drops down to -70°C (-94°F).

Hunting

Excellent hearing allows them to track their pray. They jumps high and dive headfirst into the snow.

Eyesight

Arctic foxes have excellent sense of smell and hearing but poor sight.

Arctic Fox VS Red Fox

Climate change has allowed red foxes to expand their geographic range, which lead to overlap with arctic foxes habitat. Unfortunately those two species cannot coexist in one ecosystem due to the same diet they share. This leads to arctic foxes being prayed by their red natives who weight approximately twice as them. Even thought arctic foxes are better adapted to the arctic if in an area red foxes and their white natives population is too high, then the arctic foxes number is more likely to drop down.
