Snowy Owl

Unlike most of the owls this one copes with extreme environment. Snowy owls are native to the arctic, have thick fur and noble appearance. The magnificent look and ability to survive in extremely low temperatures makes them majestic yet still very cute.

Gender

It's easy to tell a snowy owl's gender. Grown males have a very few or none black marks, unlike females whose feathers have a lot of them.

Lifespan

About 8-9 years in the wild. Snowy owls in captivity can live for more than 20 years.

Weight

Snowy owls are the heaviest owls found in North America. They weight between 700-2950 grams (3.5 to 6.5 lbs).

Activeness

Snowy owls are diurnal. This means that they can hunt during both day and night.

Airports

Snowy owls are often found hanging out at airports. It's suggested that wide open spaces remind them of the tundra.

Range

They can be found in the northern parts of Alaska, Canada, Iceland, Scandinavia, Russia and Greenland. It's common to migrate southern during the winter.

Size

Up to 70 cm height and 150 cm wingspan which makes them one of the largest owl's species globally. Females are slightly larger than males.

Diet

Their favourite pray is lemming but they would eat other small mammals, birds and fish.

Breeding

Enemies

During nesting period youngsters can be prayed by arctic foxes and wolves. Adult snowy owl can be prayed only by human.

Population

It's estimated that snowy old global population is between 200 000 and 300 000.

Speed

Snowy owls can reach speeds exceeding 80 km/hr 50 miles per hour (50 miles per hour), but are knows for their slow and quiet flight.
